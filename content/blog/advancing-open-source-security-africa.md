I am incredibly excited to announce that I will be speaking at the inaugural OSSAfrica virtual event, **"Advancing Open Source Security in Africa: OpenSSF and OSSAfrica."** Open source is the backbone of modern technology, but securing it requires a coordinated, global effort. As the Security Lead for OSSAfrica, I am deeply passionate about ensuring our local developer communities are not just consuming open-source tools, but actively participating in securing and shaping them.

### What to Expect

Hosted in collaboration with members of the Open Source Security Foundation (OpenSSF), this session is designed to demystify open-source security. Whether you are a student, a seasoned developer, or representing an organization, we will be covering:

* **The Foundations:** What open-source security actually means in today's threat landscape.
* **Current Trends:** The latest shifts and challenges in the ecosystem.
* **Actionable Pathways:** How developers and institutions across Africa can meaningfully contribute to and benefit from the global open-source movement.

### Sharing the Stage

I am honored to be joining a fantastic panel of experts and community leaders for this discussion:

* **David Wheeler** – Director and Fellow, Linux Foundation
* **Marcela Melara** – Research Scientist, Intel Labs
* **Abigail Mesrenyame Dogbe** – PhD Candidate, University of Cincinnati
* **Prince Oforh Asiedu** (Host) – Security Software Engineer, OSSAfrica

It's going to be a dynamic conversation filled with practical insights into how open collaboration strengthens software security across the board.
